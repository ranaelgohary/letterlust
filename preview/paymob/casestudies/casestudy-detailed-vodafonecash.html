    Vodafone Cash - Mobile Wallet

    MORE CASE STUDIES

    Solution Name: Mobile Wallet
    Case Study Name: Vodafone Cash
    Solution Provider: Vodafone
    Implementation Status:
    ○ live
    ○ Launch date: April 2016
    ○ 6 Million Users

    Current challenges in the market:

    The Egyptian market has one of the lowest bank penetration rates in the world and hence has to bare the high costs of cash, which includes transporting cash to and from banking points, security and insurance costs, cash tracking and cash flow. This leaves businesses with high turnover percentages and poor client experience caused by limited payment options for consumers.

    Solution Overview:

    The mobile wallet is an interoperable modular white label solution that caters for the needs of the unbanked and underserved. Paymob has worked with Vodafone alongside Mastercard and EBC (Egyptian Banks Company) on offering a financial tool that seamlessly fits into the everyday life of the Egyptian citizen, all while increasing top line and decreasing running costs. The solution serves as a tool for both the mobile wallet issuer as well as user through the following functionalities: funding, payments, transfers, collection and distribution.

    Through the (creation) of Vodafone Cash, users can receive funds on their mobile wallets through agents, ATMs, aggregators, bank accounts and bulk disbursements (government-to-person) enabling peer-to-peer transfers as well as cashing out. The mobile wallet has also been built to support direct merchant and biller integrations, in-store purchases, QR scanning, payments for financial institutions and businesses running on a collection-based model.

    Benefits & Impact on financial inclusion:

    ▪ Risk: Ensuring that e-money is going in the right flow, minimizing fraud and theft cases.
    ▪ Cost: Cuts costs for indirect/hidden fees, related to the Cash management in terms of transportation, security & actual handling.
    ▪ Technology Opportunities: Having an e-money platform opens the door for payment solutions innovations from catering to consumer features & enhanced experiences to enhanced merchant solutions covering the actual payment acceptance to reconciliation & monitoring processes.
    ▪ Revenue Opportunities:
    ○ Having an e-money based ecosystem increases the revenue as it contributes to cutting recurring costs.
    ○ Availing collection features in a more convenient manner which contributes to the collection model commitment.
    ○ Availing Disbursements, which cuts costs in terms of actually going on site to receive the amounts, also from a distribution perspective in actually handling the physical cash.
    ○ Having a fast, guaranteed reconciliation cycle.
    ○ Reducing having physical cash on premise, which lowers the risk of theft or fraud
    ○ Tracking spending allowing more effective money management.
    ○ Greater efficiencies and reduced operational costs.
    ○ Citizens to use electronic financial services which would lead to financial inclusion.
