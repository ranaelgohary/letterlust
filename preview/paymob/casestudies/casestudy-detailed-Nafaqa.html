    Nafaqa Government-To-Person

    MORE CASE STUDIES

    Case Study Name: Nafaqa Government-To-Person (G2P) Social Disbursement Program
    Solution Providers: Ministry of Supply and Internal Trade (MSIT), Bank Nasser, the four telecom operators Implementation Status: Launched October 2017, more than 11,000 women registered users

    Current challenges in the market:

    ‘Nafaqa’ is the first digital government-to-person (G2P) program in Egypt. This digital social transfer solution aims to facilitate the disbursement of alimony allowances (previously disbursed in cash) to about 390,000 widows or divorced Egyptian women. Nasser Bank launched this service in October 2017, in partnership with the mobile operators in Egypt. Five months after the program launch, Nasser bank reported the registration of more than 11,000 women who had applied for the benefit payment service, thus not only proving the fast scale, but also the consumer appetite for such initiatives.

    Interestingly, the mobile social transfer service not only allows women to collect their allowances, but to also to pay their expenses (electricity bills, airtime top-ups etc.) directly through their mobile wallets. This digital solution spares a lot of time and hassles for the alimony beneficiaries by availing a greater number of channels (over 90k branch, hub and outlet), which means these women no longer have to queue for long hours to get their allowances. In addition to developing and maintaining the Vodafone Cash platform, PayMob designed and developed a fully compliant solution for bulk disbursements. This tool is used by Nasser Bank to disburse monthly allowances to the Vodafone Cash’s nafaqa users, but the same tool could also be used for any other mass disbursement transactions (payrolls or other forms of social funds, like pensions). Paymob was also instrumental to the design-thinking of the Social Fund’s use cases and consumer journey: everything from how Nafaqa’s beneficiaries register to how they get and spend their money has been mapped by Paymob along with the ecosystem major players (MNO & Naser Bank) under the supervision & support of the Central Bank of Egypt.

    The digital Nafaqa program provides an entry-point to the financial inclusion of the previously largely unbanked social transfer recipients through the use of a more efficient and safe payment instrument. On the long run, this solution will also allow to bundle social transfers with savings or transaction accounts.
