    AUC Coin - Cashless Community

    Solution Provider: American University of Cairo (AUC)

    American University of Cairo (AUC) is the first university in Egypt to adopt the cashless initiative and apply it across all on campus across campus services and third-party outlets. In addition to developing and maintaining the cashless solution implemented on AUC premises, Paymob constantly works on developing and enhancing the campus payments experience and constantly works on adding different use cases to increase the ultimate benefit of the community. This implementation has proven the benefits and feasibility of such solution to other closed communities such as, schools, workspaces and other residential communities. Paymob was also instrumental in contributing to the design-thinking of the whole end-to-end experience catered for a better customer journey.

    Vodafone Cash - Mobile Wallet

    Solution Provider: Vodafone

    The mobile wallet is an interoperable modular white label solution that caters for the needs of the unbanked and underserved. Paymob has worked with Vodafone alongside Mastercard and EBC (Egyptian Banks Company) on offering a financial tool that seamlessly fits into the everyday life of the Egyptian citizen, all while increasing top line and decreasing running costs. The solution serves as a tool for both the mobile wallet issuer as well as user through the following functionalities: funding, payments, transfers, collection and distribution.

    MFI Gateway Solution

    Solution Provider: Alexandria Business Association (ABA), three Telecom Operators (Orange, Etisalat, Vodafone)

    Paymob’s MFI “Gateway solution” aims to provide an entry point for microfinance customers to get access to an increased range of financial services, by capitalising on mobile wallets. With more than 400,000 active borrowers across 7 governorates and more than 60 branches, Alexandria Businessmen Association (ABA) is the MFI in Egypt with the largest outreach in terms of number of customers. As of early 2019, ABA became the first MFI to launch a digital solution allowing their customers to receive loans and pay installments through mobile wallets. This solution was launched in partnership with Vodafone, Egypt’s leading mobile network operator. By providing the integration between the MNO and ABA, Paymob managed to automate the collection of loan installments for ABA through Vodafone.

    Nafaqa Government-To-Person

    Solution Provider: Ministry of Supply and Internal Trade (MSIT), Bank Nasser, the four telecom operators

    The digital Nafaqa program provides an entry-point to the financial inclusion of the previously largely unbanked social transfer recipients through the use of a more efficient and safe payment instrument. On the long run, this solution will also allow to bundle social transfers with savings or transaction accounts.
